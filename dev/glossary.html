Glossary ​

Breeder's equation

A genetics rule: if tolerance is heritable, survivors' offspring will be more tolerant on average. In Kora this is a model assumption, not a confirmed GBR outcome.

Heritability ()

means roughly 30% of the phenotypic variance in thermal tolerance across the population is attributable to additive genetic effects. The remaining variance reflects environmental, symbiont, and stochastic contributions. All traits are inherited; heritability is not a statement about whether a trait is inherited but about what fraction of population-level variation in that trait covaries with additive genetic relatedness.

Truncated-normal tolerance distribution

A bell-curve of heat tolerance across colonies, clipped so no negative values occur.

Self-seeding proportion

The fraction of larvae settling on the same reef where they were produced.

Ensemble simulation

Running the model many times with different inputs to map how outcomes vary across the scenario space. In a DMDU context, the goal is coverage of plausible conditions rather than sampling from a known probability distribution.

Depth coefficient

A factor reducing bleaching impact on deeper corals. The effect decreases as background ocean temperatures rise.

DHW (Degree Heating Weeks)

Accumulated thermal anomaly above the monthly mean maximum, in degrees C times weeks. NOAA Coral Reef Watch DHW accumulates only in weeks where SST exceeds the MMM by at least 1 degree C. Weeks below this threshold contribute zero to the running total.

Deep uncertainty

Conditions under which reliable probabilities cannot be assigned to future states. In reef management, the future pace of warming, the degree of in-situ thermal adaptation, and larval connectivity between reefs at management-relevant spatial scales are genuinely unknown rather than merely imprecisely estimated.

Scenario Discovery

A method for identifying the specific conditions (climate trajectories, parameter values, deployment scales) under which a management strategy succeeds or fails. Applied to Kora ensemble output, it answers the question: under what climate and ecological conditions does this deployment plan achieve the target outcome?

Robustness

The property of a strategy that performs acceptably across a wide range of plausible futures rather than being optimised for a single expected outcome. Robustness is the primary design target for strategies developed with Kora.

Scenario space

The multi-dimensional space defined by uncertain inputs such as DHW scenario, heritability, and deployment scale. Each run in an ensemble samples one point in this space.

Percentile ribbon

In an ensemble plot, the band between the 10th and 90th percentile outcomes. It represents the spread of model outcomes across the scenario space, not a statistical confidence interval, because no sampling distribution is assumed.
